## Strategic Priorities

### 1. Nail the positioning: AI dev shop for AI consultants

The pivot from "SME service businesses broadly" to "AI consultants specifically" is the current strategic bet. AI consultants:
- Already believe in AI (no education sale required)
- Have clients who need implementation (built-in distribution)
- Are a more reachable, tighter audience than the general SME market
- Likely make their own buying decisions

The work now is to validate this ICP, build one strong case study with an AI consultant client, and develop a repeatable offer around it.

### 2. Build content as the primary lead channel

LinkedIn is the chosen channel. The strategy is to publish consistently on:
- Process thinking as the critical skill in the AI age
- AI management: being the director/orchestrator, letting AI handle execution
- Lessons from building real systems (honest, experience-backed, no guru posturing)

The AI LinkedIn content system is being built internally — using AI to assist production so posting is consistent regardless of energy or context-switching pressure.

Content angle: **process-first, data-grounded, philosophically honest**. Not tool tutorials. Not "here's my exact method." The hook is thinking, not tactics.

### 3. Land the first proper client through warm channels

The path to first client is warm: network, LinkedIn content attracting inbound, or referrals from the AI consultant community.

Target: one real client using the 3-stage AI Operating Layer framework. Build the case study. Then use it as proof for the next.

### 4. Build the internal AI workflow stack

You're using yourself as the first client. Goal: AI handles as much execution as possible (content drafting, research, reporting) so your role becomes review, direction, and decision — not production.

This is both a business necessity (solo founder bandwidth) and a proof-of-concept (if you can't run your own business on AI workflows, you can't sell that to clients).

## Key Decisions or Open Questions

1. **ICP lock-in**: Is "AI consultants" the right primary audience, or is it a stepping stone to their SME clients? Need to validate whether AI consultants buy implementation or refer it.

2. **Offer structure**: Is the AI Operating Layer (3-stage, multi-year engagement) the right entry point, or does a smaller, faster-to-deliver offer (e.g., "process audit + automation blueprint") work better for getting first clients across the line?

3. **Content → commercial bridge**: LinkedIn builds awareness and trust. What's the explicit next step for someone who resonates with the content — strategy call, lead magnet, something else?

4. **Outreach approach**: Warm network vs. inbound-only vs. targeted cold outreach to AI consultants specifically.
