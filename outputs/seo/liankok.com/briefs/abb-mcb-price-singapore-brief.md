### H2: What Is the Difference Between 6kA and 10kA MCBs? (250–300 words)
- **Answer-first**: Breaking capacity (kA) is the maximum fault current an MCB can safely interrupt. A 6kA MCB can handle a fault current of up to 6,000 amperes before tripping; a 10kA MCB handles up to 10,000A. For most Singapore HDB flats, 6kA is sufficient. For commercial premises and industrial applications, 10kA is standard.
- Cover: Singapore HDB distribution board typical fault levels; when each is required; cost premium of 10kA (~20–30% more); common misconception (higher kA ≠ better for simple HDB use)
- **Chart 2**: Comparison grouped bar — 6kA vs 10kA price premium across 1P, 2P, 3P configurations
- **Key stat**: Singapore's HDB supply voltage is 230V single-phase; typical prospective short-circuit current at HDB DB boards is 3–6kA — making 6kA MCBs technically sufficient for most residential installations

### H2: MCB Curve Types Explained: B, C, D for Singapore Applications (250–300 words)
- **Answer-first**: The trip curve determines how quickly the MCB responds to overcurrent. Curve B trips at 3–5× rated current (lighting circuits, resistive loads); Curve C at 5–10× (general power sockets, motors with moderate inrush); Curve D at 10–20× (equipment with heavy inrush — motors, transformers, welding).
- Cover: Which curve is correct for Singapore HDB (Curve C standard); which for commercial lighting (Curve B); which for industrial (Curve D); common installation mistake (using Curve D where Curve C is correct, meaning slower protection)
- **Image**: ABB curve type diagram illustration (use official ABB technical diagram or Unsplash: "electrical engineering diagram")

### FAQ Section (5 items)
1. **How much does it cost to replace an MCB in my HDB flat?** — MCB unit cost + electrician labour (licensed electrician required under Singapore EMA regulations): total typical cost S$100–S$180 for a single MCB replacement.
2. **Can I replace my own MCB in Singapore?** — No. Under the Singapore Electricity Act, all electrical works in HDB flats and private residences must be carried out by a Licensed Electrical Worker (LEW). Lian Kok can supply the MCB; your LEW installs it.
3. **What does the ampere rating mean on an MCB?** — The ampere rating (e.g., 20A, 32A) is the maximum continuous current the MCB will carry without tripping. For HDB lighting circuits, 6A or 10A is standard; for power sockets, 20A or 32A.
4. **Are ABB MCBs compatible with other brands of DB boards?** — Yes. ABB S200 series MCBs are DIN rail-mounted and compatible with all standard 35mm DIN rail distribution boards. They are not brand-exclusive.
5. **Do ABB MCBs come with a warranty?** — Yes. Genuine ABB products purchased from an authorised distributor like Lian Kok carry ABB's manufacturer warranty. Grey market or unverified sources cannot provide manufacturer warranty support.
